## What is Cauldron?

Cauldron puts the latest tools from the world of AI into the hands of the scientists
who move drug discovery forward — **pharmacologists, medicinal chemists, and
structural biologists**. Proteins, sequences, and small molecules in one pot, driven
by a conversation instead of a pile of scripts, notebooks, and disconnected viewers.

The frontier of AI for biology is moving faster than any single lab can track.
Cauldron is where those tools land — usable, together, on your bench.

## Your data stays yours

Cauldron is **local-first**. Models run **on your own machine**; your structures,
sequences, and notes never leave it unless you say so. Want a frontier cloud model?
Bring your own key. Want everything offline? That works too. Privacy isn't a
setting you switch on — it's the default.

## Scientists and companies, in one pot

We're building the place where **scientists and companies meet**. In this new age, a
single scientist can do more, and faster — and, with our partners, cheaper. The
instruments that used to demand a team, a budget, and a long queue are becoming a
conversation.

## Get it

Grab the latest installer from the [**Releases**](https://github.com/chesseli/cauldron-releases/releases/latest) page:

| OS | File | Install |
|----|------|---------|
| **Windows** | `Cauldron-Setup-<version>.exe` | Double-click the installer. |
| **Linux (Debian/Ubuntu)** | `cauldron_<version>_amd64.deb` | `sudo apt install ./cauldron_<version>_amd64.deb` |
| **Linux (portable)** | `Cauldron-<version>.AppImage` | `chmod +x` and run — no install. |
| **macOS** | *coming soon* | — |

Installed apps auto-update from this repository (AppImage + Windows). This repo holds
**release artifacts only** — the application source lives in a private repository.
